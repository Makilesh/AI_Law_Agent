COMMONLY REFERENCED IPC SECTIONS - INDIAN PENAL CODE

SECTION 302 - MURDER
Offense: Intentional killing of another person
Penalty: Death penalty or life imprisonment and fine
Elements: Intent to cause death, act causing death, victim dies
Notes: One of the gravest offenses under IPC
Bail: Generally non-bailable

SECTION 304 - CULPABLE HOMICIDE NOT AMOUNTING TO MURDER
Offense: Causing death without intention to kill
Part I: Life imprisonment or up to 10 years imprisonment and fine
Part II: Up to 10 years imprisonment and/or fine
Notes: Lesser degree than murder, depends on intention and knowledge

SECTION 307 - ATTEMPT TO MURDER
Offense: Attempting to cause death of another person
Penalty: Up to 10 years imprisonment and fine
If hurt caused: Life imprisonment possible
Notes: Act must be done with intention to cause death
Bail: Non-bailable offense

SECTION 376 - RAPE
Offense: Sexual intercourse without consent
Penalty: Minimum 7-10 years, can extend to life imprisonment
Aggravated cases: Minimum 10 years to life imprisonment or death
Notes: Non-bailable offense, victim identity protected
Special courts: Fast-track trials mandated

SECTION 420 - CHEATING AND DISHONESTLY INDUCING DELIVERY OF PROPERTY
Offense: Deceiving someone to gain property or valuable security
Penalty: Up to 7 years imprisonment and fine
Elements: Deception, inducement, dishonest intention, delivery of property
Examples: Fraud, fake promises, forgery in business
Bail: Bailable in most cases
Common Usage: Often cited in financial fraud, online scams, cheque bouncing

SECTION 498A - CRUELTY BY HUSBAND OR RELATIVES
Offense: Harassment or cruelty towards married woman by husband/in-laws
Penalty: Up to 3 years imprisonment and fine
Elements: Mental or physical cruelty, dowry demands
Notes: Cognizable, non-bailable offense
Scope: Includes harassment for dowry, mental torture

SECTION 354 - ASSAULT OR CRIMINAL FORCE WITH INTENT TO OUTRAGE MODESTY
Offense: Using criminal force with intent to outrage woman's modesty
Penalty: Minimum 1 year, can extend to 5 years imprisonment and fine
Notes: Non-bailable, cognizable offense
Related: Section 354A (sexual harassment), 354B (assault to disrobe)

SECTION 377 - UNNATURAL OFFENSES
Status: Partially struck down by Supreme Court in 2018
Current: Applies only to non-consensual acts and acts with minors/animals
Historical: Previously criminalized consensual same-sex relations
Notes: Landmark judgment decriminalized consensual adult relations

SECTION 406 - CRIMINAL BREACH OF TRUST
Offense: Dishonestly misappropriating property entrusted to you
Penalty: Up to 3 years imprisonment and/or fine
Elements: Entrustment, dishonest misappropriation or conversion
Examples: Employee stealing from employer, agent misusing client funds
Bail: Generally bailable

SECTION 323 - VOLUNTARILY CAUSING HURT
Offense: Causing physical hurt voluntarily
Penalty: Up to 1 year imprisonment and/or fine up to Rs. 1,000
Notes: Bailable, compoundable offense
Difference from 325: Does not cause grievous hurt

SECTION 325 - VOLUNTARILY CAUSING GRIEVOUS HURT
Offense: Causing serious physical injury
Penalty: Up to 7 years imprisonment and fine
Grievous Hurt: Fractures, permanent disfigurement, loss of organ function
Bail: Non-bailable in many cases

SECTION 379 - THEFT
Offense: Dishonestly taking movable property without consent
Penalty: Up to 3 years imprisonment and/or fine
Elements: Taking, dishonest intention, movable property, without consent
Bail: Generally bailable

SECTION 380 - THEFT IN DWELLING HOUSE
Offense: Committing theft in a building used as dwelling
Penalty: Up to 7 years imprisonment and fine
Notes: More serious than simple theft due to violation of home

SECTION 447 - CRIMINAL TRESPASS
Offense: Entering property with intent to commit offense or intimidate
Penalty: Up to 3 months imprisonment and/or fine up to Rs. 500
Notes: Bailable offense

SECTION 452 - HOUSE TRESPASS AFTER PREPARATION FOR HURT
Offense: Trespassing with intent to cause hurt
Penalty: Up to 7 years imprisonment and fine
Notes: Non-bailable, more serious than simple trespass

SECTION 504 - INTENTIONAL INSULT WITH INTENT TO PROVOKE BREACH OF PEACE
Offense: Intentionally insulting someone to provoke them
Penalty: Up to 2 years imprisonment and/or fine
Notes: Bailable offense, commonly used

SECTION 506 - CRIMINAL INTIMIDATION
Offense: Threatening someone with injury to person, reputation, or property
Penalty: Up to 2 years imprisonment and/or fine
With death threat: Up to 7 years imprisonment
Notes: Commonly used in threat cases

GENERAL NOTES:
- IPC enacted in 1860, still primary criminal law code
- Bailable offenses: Accused can get bail as a right
- Non-bailable: Court discretion required for bail
- Cognizable: Police can arrest without warrant
- Non-cognizable: Police need warrant to arrest
- Compoundable: Can be settled between parties
