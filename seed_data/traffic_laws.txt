INDIAN TRAFFIC LAWS AND PENALTIES - MOTOR VEHICLES ACT 1988

HELMET VIOLATIONS (Section 129)
Offense: Riding without helmet or wearing non-standard helmet
Penalty: First offense - Rs. 1,000 fine and/or 3 months imprisonment
Subsequent offenses: Rs. 2,000 fine and/or 6 months imprisonment
Notes: Both rider and pillion passenger must wear ISI marked helmets
Exemptions: Sikhs wearing turbans are exempt under certain conditions

SEATBELT VIOLATIONS (Section 138)
Offense: Not wearing seatbelt while driving
Penalty: Rs. 1,000 fine
Notes: Applies to both driver and all passengers
Mandatory for both front and rear seat passengers

DRUNK DRIVING (Section 185)
Offense: Driving under influence of alcohol or drugs
BAC Limit: 30 mg per 100 ml of blood
Penalty: First offense - Rs. 10,000 fine and/or 6 months imprisonment
Subsequent offenses: Rs. 15,000 fine and/or 2 years imprisonment
License: Suspension for 6 months or more

SPEEDING VIOLATIONS (Section 183)
Offense: Exceeding prescribed speed limits
Light Motor Vehicle: Rs. 1,000-2,000 fine
Medium/Heavy Vehicle: Rs. 2,000-4,000 fine
Notes: Speed limits vary by vehicle type and road type
Repeat offense within 3 years can lead to license suspension

TRAFFIC SIGNAL VIOLATIONS (Section 177)
Offense: Jumping red light or disobeying traffic signals
Penalty: Rs. 1,000-5,000 fine
Repeat offense: Rs. 10,000 fine and possible license suspension
Notes: Considered a serious offense with potential for accidents

DRIVING WITHOUT LICENSE (Section 181)
Offense: Driving without valid license
Penalty: Rs. 5,000 fine and/or imprisonment up to 3 months
Notes: Includes expired, suspended, or invalid licenses
Vehicle may be impounded

DRIVING WITHOUT INSURANCE (Section 196)
Offense: Operating vehicle without valid insurance
Penalty: Rs. 2,000 fine for first offense
Subsequent offenses: Rs. 4,000 fine and/or 3 months imprisonment
Notes: Third party insurance is mandatory by law

USING MOBILE PHONE WHILE DRIVING (Section 177)
Offense: Using mobile phone without hands-free device
Penalty: Rs. 1,000-5,000 fine
Repeat offense: Higher penalties and potential license suspension
Notes: Includes texting, calling, or using apps while driving

OVERLOADING (Section 194)
Offense: Carrying excess passengers or goods
Light vehicles: Rs. 2,000 per extra passenger/100kg
Heavy vehicles: Rs. 20,000 plus Rs. 2,000 per extra ton
Notes: Can lead to vehicle seizure

DANGEROUS DRIVING (Section 184)
Offense: Rash or negligent driving endangering public safety
Penalty: Rs. 1,000-5,000 fine and/or 6 months-1 year imprisonment
Serious cases: Rs. 10,000 fine and up to 2 years imprisonment
Notes: Can include racing, stunts, or aggressive driving

PARKING VIOLATIONS
Offense: Illegal parking or obstruction
Penalty: Rs. 500-1,000 fine
No-parking zones: Vehicle may be towed at owner's expense
Notes: Varies by municipal corporation rules

MINOR DRIVING (Section 199A)
Offense: Allowing minor to drive
Penalty for guardian: Rs. 25,000 fine and/or 3 years imprisonment
License cancellation: Registration may be cancelled
Notes: Legal driving age is 18 years (16 for two-wheelers below 50cc with parental consent)

GENERAL NOTES:
- All fines are as per Motor Vehicles (Amendment) Act, 2019
- State governments may impose additional penalties
- Traffic violations can lead to points on license
- Multiple violations can result in license suspension
- Repeat offenders face higher penalties
- Some violations are compoundable, others require court appearance
- Payment must be made within 60 days to avoid additional charges
